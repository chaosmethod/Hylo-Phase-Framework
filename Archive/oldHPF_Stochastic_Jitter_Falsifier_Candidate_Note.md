## Abstract

HPF predicts that finite scheduler discreteness and vacuum transport granularity can produce an irreducible propagation-induced arrival-time dispersion for sufficiently energetic photons over sufficiently long baselines. The leading candidate prediction is not a coherent linear lag, but a stochastic broadening floor with near-zero mean shift. The natural primitive scale is the scheduler tick
\[
t_{sched}=\frac{L_{vac}}{c},
\]
with \(L_{vac}\) the active substrate resolution scale. Under the current canon, the leading accumulation law is a gated random-walk form,
\[
\sigma_t(E,D;S_f)\sim t_{sched}\sqrt{N_{\rm eff}(E,D,S_f)}\,\Gamma(S_f;b,\eta),
\]
not a deterministic \(N\,t_{sched}\) drift. The effect is predicted to be negligible in low-load coherent regimes and to turn on only when the propagation path samples the instability-active corridor. This note states the candidate mechanism, separates propagation jitter from source and detector noise, and gives explicit kill conditions.

HPF does **not** merely say that high-energy photon arrival times “may be noisy.” The candidate falsifier is sharper:

> **Candidate prediction.** HPF predicts either:
>

The observable is **positive excess broadening** in arrival-time distributions, with approximately zero leading-order mean shift. The note therefore targets variance, not compulsory signed delay. This separation matters because a coherent linear lag and a stochastic broadening floor are different physical claims.

## 3. Mechanism

The proposed mechanism is:

1. **Finite scheduler discreteness.** Propagation is not executed on a continuum with arbitrarily fine temporal resolution. The substrate primitive is \(t_{sched}=L_{vac}/c\).

2. **Vacuum transport granularity.** Even in the low-load vacuum sector, transport is executed through a finite-resolution substrate rather than an infinitely smooth continuum. The vacuum-catastrophe note already isolates \(L_{vac}\) as the relevant legality scale and explicitly splits the stochastic jitter lane into a separate obligation set.

3. **Cumulative micro-variance.** Microscopic propagation slips need not add with the same sign. Under current canon, nothing supplies a pathwise same-sign coherence law for those slips. The square-root transfer structure already active in the governor map points the other way: support-like quantities feed availability-like execution through a one-step square-root conversion. That favors variance accumulation over coherent linear drift.

4. **Regime gating.** The effect is not predicted to be universally active at the same strength. It should be gated by the same phase structure that already controls blur onset, entropy onset, and high-load execution behavior through \(\{S_{\rm blur},\eta,b\}\) and the \(k\propto 1/b\) sharp-gate structure.

Hence the candidate observable is a propagation-induced stochastic floor, not an obligatory deterministic lag.

## 4. Leading branch choice

The current canon supports the branch

\[
\boxed{\sigma_t \propto \sqrt{N_{\rm eff}}\,t_{sched}}
\]

with regime gating, rather than

\[
\sigma_t \propto N_{\rm eff}\,t_{sched}.
\]

Reason:

- a linear-in-\(N\) law would read as a coherent systematic drift,
- the current package does not provide a same-sign microscopic accumulation principle,
- the governor structure and the support/availability split favor amplitude-like transfer and variance-style accumulation instead.

So the clean candidate ansatz is:

\[
\boxed{
\sigma_t(E,D;S_f)=t_{sched}\,\sqrt{N_{\rm eff}(E,D,S_f)}\,\Gamma(S_f;b,\eta)
}
\]

where:

- \(t_{sched}=L_{vac}/c\),
- \(N_{\rm eff}\) is the effective number of independent transport domains sampled,
- \(\Gamma\) is the HPF regime gate.

## 7. Propagation jitter versus source and detector noise

Any usable note must separate three distinct objects:

### 7.1 Source-intrinsic variability

Emission-time structure native to the astrophysical source.

### 7.2 Detector timing noise

Instrumental response, timestamp uncertainty, trigger logic, event reconstruction, and binning artifacts.

### 7.3 Propagation-induced irreducible floor

A source-independent residual broadening accumulated during transit.

The HPF claim concerns **only 7.3**.

So the observable decomposition is

\[
\sigma_{\rm obs}^2
=
\sigma_{\rm src}^2
+
\sigma_{\rm det}^2
+
\sigma_{\rm prop}^2,
\]

with HPF targeting

\[
\sigma_{\rm prop}=\sigma_t(E,D;S_f).
\]

The note should explicitly state that HPF is falsified by the absence of any allowed \(\sigma_{\rm prop}\) signal once source and detector contributions are controlled to below the predicted bound.

## 8. Sign of the effect

Leading candidate sign prediction:

- **mean shift:** approximately zero
- **variance:** positive definite
- **observable consequence:** extra arrival-time broadening, not compulsory late arrival

This is one of the cleanest ways to distinguish the HPF candidate from standard one-parameter Lorentz-violation lag models, which often lead with signed group-velocity delay. The present candidate is a stochastic floor, not a monotone deterministic slowdown.

## 9. Kill conditions

HPF is killed, in this lane, if any of the following occur:

1. **No excess propagation broadening.** After credible subtraction or bounding of source and detector contributions, no propagation-induced excess remains above the HPF upper bound.

2. **Wrong distance scaling.** The measured propagation component scales incompatibly with the candidate \(\sqrt{D}\)-type accumulation law.

3. **Wrong energy behavior.** The excess broadening fails to show the predicted thresholded energy/load dependence, or shows the opposite trend.

4. **Compulsory signed lag without broadening.** A persistent signed propagation lag appears as the dominant effect while stochastic broadening does not.

5. **Inconsistent multi-source behavior.** Different long-baseline high-energy sources fail to follow any common propagation law once source and detector effects are separated.

Those are the right kill conditions because they attack the propagation claim itself rather than letting the note hide behind source astrophysics.
